Disconnections

Members undertake all responsibility to fully comprehend and understand all rules and regulations prior to taking part in any tournament – this rule applies to all matches. In a case where a game is disconnected, all efforts are made to ensure accurate recreation to the point of disconnection, so that play is resumed with the correct score and remaining time. It is expected that all users/players will exit a game once the time limit has been played out. However, if no such exit takes place, any more time played will be considered valid and added to the final score. Point spreads shall be added to the total score as per our rules, unless the players have agreed to other verifiable conditions prior to the game. Any game not fully played shall be subjected to review. Any description of how a game should be played/completed needs to be dealt with before starting the actual match. If a new game is initiated based on the intention to play out the remaining time and issues are raised with teams, the ball, the spot of the ball, player injuries etc, then you should immediately exit and present supporting evidence. However, if the game goes on, then your claim shall not be validated. Actions intimated to go against our disconnection policy during a game which you are losing shall result in an automatic forfeit. Following our disconnection policy is integral. And refusal to comply with these rules whilst you are losing or tied, will lead to automatic forfeit from a game. Even in cases where you are winning and do not respond to our disconnection policy, you shall be liable for an unfavorable decision. We do not force re-matches or re-schedules; therefore if you are unable to comply within reasonable time lines (5-10 minutes), then it shall result in a forfeit. We reserve the right to forfeit a game in a disconnection scenario, where the circumstances do not cater to the remaining time and the afore-played situation cannot be wholly recreated. In some situations, a player can be kicked out for “excessive grieving”, which should be treated as disconnection unless supporting evidence against it can be provided. Getting kicked out for excessive grieving whilst losing a match results in forfeiting the time, while if you do the same when winning, it leads to an unfavorable decision.
